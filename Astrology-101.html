What is Astrology?

Astrology is the ancient study of the movements and positions of celestial bodies—such as the stars, planets, and moon—and how they influence human lives and natural events on Earth.

Astrology is not just about horoscopes—it's a symbolic language that uses the position of planets at your time of birth to reflect your inner self and your journey through life.

Fun Fact: The word “zodiac” comes from the Greek “zōidiakos,” meaning “circle of animals.” Eleven of the twelve zodiac signs are represented by animals!

Signs, Elements, and Modalities

The twelve zodiac signs are divided among four natural elements: Fire, Earth, Air and Water. Each element shares core emotional and psychological traits that influence how signs express themselves.


Beyond elements, zodiac signs are also classified by modality, which reveals how a sign interacts with change: Cardinal signs start seasons and initiate action, Fixed signs are stable and persistent, and Mutable signs are flexible and adaptable.


Fun Fact: Virgo is the only zodiac sign represented by a human female figure—the maiden!

What is a Birth Chart?

A birth chart, also known as a natal chart, is a snapshot of the sky at the exact moment and location of your birth. It maps the positions of the planets in the twelve astrological houses and signs, forming the foundation of personal astrology. Each component of the chart tells a different story—from your Sun sign (your core identity), Moon sign (your emotions), to your Ascendant (how others see you).

Fun Fact: No two birth charts are exactly alike—not even for twins born minutes apart!

Astrology vs. Astronomy

Though they both study celestial bodies, astrology and astronomy are different disciplines. Astronomy is a natural science focused on the physics and movement of objects in space. Astrology, on the other hand, is a metaphysical study that interprets how these celestial patterns influence human lives and consciousness.


Historically, astrology and astronomy were part of the same field. Over time, they diverged as astronomy became more evidence-based and astrology remained symbolic and interpretive.


Fun Fact: Many famous astronomers in history—like Galileo and Kepler—were also astrologers!

Astrological Compatibility

Compatibility in astrology goes beyond Sun signs. While it’s popular to say “Aries and Leo are a perfect match,” deeper compatibility readings include the Moon, Venus, and Mars placements, as well as house overlays. These components reveal how two individuals communicate, show affection, and deal with challenges together.


Fun Fact: Synastry—the comparison of two birth charts—is the astrological technique used to explore compatibility!
